The Moultrie Mobile story

In 1979, Dan Moultrie built his first spin-cast feeder to prevent deer from merely passing through his property. The feeder was so effective that his friends asked Dan to build feeders for them too. That led to the birth of the brand — Moultrie Mobile. What started as a solution to a problem evolved into an innovative strategy: creating the best scouting tools on the market. Moultrie Mobile went on to pioneer industry-first innovations, from cellular technology to integrated memory and onboard AI.

“We take care of our customers… we don’t forget who the customer is.” — Dan Moultrie

The app

The Moultrie Mobile app is more than trail camera photos — it’s a hunt-planning tool designed to give you the edge. With features that go beyond deer pictures, the app provides what you need to make informed, data-driven decisions in the field.

  • Connected devices — Cameras, connected feeders, and power accessories managed from one app; control settings and get alerts anytime, anywhere.
  • Interactive maps — Street, satellite, and terrain; mark access points, draw lines and shapes, measure distances; ideal wind forecasts; topo and property lines.
  • Game Plan — Local weather and trail camera data to predict the best times to be in the field; 10-day forecast, Hunt Score, peak activity times.
  • Gallery & Buck List — Albums, smart tags (bucks, turkeys, people, vehicles), HD download, shared galleries; name and track your Buck List.
  • Moultrie AI — Focus on specific targets or exclude zones; reduce unwanted shots like waving branches.
  • Activity charts — By time of day, day of week, moon phase, temperature; trends tied to photo albums.

In the community

Moultrie Mobile is committed to the communities we serve. For 12+ years, Dan Moultrie sat on the State of Alabama’s Department of Conservation Advisory Board, helping guide policy in conserving natural resources. He has helped contribute and raise over one million dollars for organizations such as Hunters Helping the Hungry and The Governor’s One Shot Turkey Hunt. The Auburn University Wildlife and Forestry School has named the Dan Moultrie Fund for Excellence Scholarship in his honor. We donate 5% of before-tax profits to causes and organizations that improve quality of life in our communities.
